📝 My Weekly Peace Audit

Every Friday, I reflect on these three questions to keep my heart aligned with peace.

📍 Where did I show a heart of peace this week?

Example from last week: I stayed calm when a user was frustrated on a call. I listened for 10 minutes without interrupting and resolved their issue.

⚔️ Where did a heart of war appear?

Example: I felt annoyed during a long training session when a student asked the same question three times. I noticed my impatience rising.

🔄 What will I do differently next week?

Action plan: I will take a deep breath before responding to difficult questions and remember how I struggled to learn IT concepts as a beginner.

"In your patience possess ye your souls." — Luke 21:19

⚖️ My Ethical Dilemma Reflection

Scenario: An IT administrator accessed employee payroll and medical records without permission. He was "just curious" about a coworker's salary.

Why this is wrong:

  • It breaks trust. Employees trust IT staff to protect their private data.
  • It treats people as objects. Viewing private information without consent disrespects the person's dignity.
  • Small wrongs lead to bigger wrongs. If I peek today, I might be tempted to steal or manipulate data tomorrow.

My commitment: I will never abuse admin privileges. If tempted, I will ask myself: "Would I want someone doing this to my mother's medical records?"

🙏 Personal Lesson: Integrity Matters — IT professionals handle private data and critical systems. Honesty and integrity build trust and protect people and organizations.

⚠️ My Personal Warning Signs (When I Slip into a "Heart of War")

Defensiveness

When it happens: Someone challenges my technical solution or questions my decision.

How I will fix it: I will pause 10 seconds and say, "Help me understand your perspective better."

Impatience

When it happens: A user learns slowly or asks the same question twice.

How I will fix it: I will remember how I struggled to learn IT concepts at the cybercafe and extend the same grace I received.

"Us vs. Them" thinking

When it happens: I see non-technical users as obstacles or "problems" rather than people.

How I will fix it: I will remind myself each user is a child of God with unique fears and hopes.

Cutting corners

When it happens: I am tempted to skip a security step or documentation to save time.

How I will fix it: I will re-read my ethical dilemma reflection and remember that shortcuts today become bad habits tomorrow.
