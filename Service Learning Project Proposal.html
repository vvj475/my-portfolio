Service Learning Project Proposal

Project Description/Objective

[Explain a problem in your community that you want to address in a 12-hour service-learning project. Describe how you will execute this project and how the skills you have gained in your education will help address this problem?]

Problem in my community:

Many youth in my community are ignorant about the benefits of technology and adamant about  avoid in it. They prefer outdated methods for studying, communicating, and problem-solving,   which puts them at a disadvantage in a digital world.

 

How I will address this in a 12-hour project:

 I will organize a 2-day "Tech Skill for Life" workshop (2 hours planning, 10 hours execution).The work-shop will include interactive sessions showing how technology simplifies schoolwork,  job searching,   and daily tasks; using free online tools (Google Docs, Canva, Khan Academy);     and a Q&A panel with young adults who successfully used technology for education and small   business.

 

Skills from my education that will help:

 Digital literacy and research skills, communication skills to explain complex tech simply, and    problem-solving to address their fears (e.g., privacy, difficulty).

 

Target Audience

[Who is being affected by this problem? How will your project help them?]

Who is affected:

Youth ages 12–18 in my local community who avoid technology due to lack of exposure, or negative      influences.

 

 

 

How my project will help them:

The workshop will make tech less scary, teach basic digital skills, and give them reasons to use technology for school, jobs, and everyday life. They’ll also meet mentors who can keep helping them after the workshop.

Team Members

[List the members of the team you built in the Teamwork & Leadership CWC Challenge. Also include people in your community who can contribute to making  your project successful success and explain how.]

1. Mr. Michael Okonkwo - From Teamwork & Leadership CWC Challenge; responsible for   logistics and scheduling.

2. Mrs Patricia Thomas -  From Teamwork & Leadership CWC Challenge; responsible for creating      simple handouts  and visual aids.

3. Local Librarian/Teacher: Provides space and recommends trustworthy free tech resources.

4. The community youth president: Shares personal success story using tech for work/study.

 

Technology Needed

[Identify the technology you will need to complete this project.]

Laptop and projector, free Wi-Fi access, smartphones (participants can bring their own), free online tools (Google Drive, YouTube), social media (WhatsApp, Instagram).

Steps to Completion

[List the steps you will take to complete this project:]

1. Get approval from teacher on this proposal.

2. Contact local library or community center to reserve a room.

3. Recruit team members and assign roles.

4. Create workshop agenda and simple handouts.

5. Promote event via social media and flyers.

6. Prepare presentations and hands-on activities.

7. Run the 2-day workshop (5 hours each day).

8. Collect feedback forms to measure success.

9. Submit final reflection to teacher.

College-Wide Capabilities

[Describe how you will build Professionalism, Problem-solving, Communication, Teamwork & Leadership, and Technical Skills with this project.]

1. Professionalism: Arrive on time, dress appropriately, communicate respectfully.

2. Problem-solving: Adapt activities if participants struggle; address tech fears patiently.

3. Communication: Explain tech concepts in simple, encouraging language.

4. Teamwork & Leadership: Delegate tasks, lead group discussions, resolve conflicts.

5. Technical Skills: Use presentation software, online tools, and troubleshoot basic device issues.

 

Disciple Leadership

[Describe how this project will help you build disciple leadership]

This project will help me build disciple leadership by serving others selflessly, following Christlike        principles of patience and empathy, and humbly using my education to lift those who are struggling. I     will  pray for guidance to see the youth as Heavenly Father sees them and to develop greater charity  andteaching ability.

 

Assessment

How will you know if the project was successful or not?

I will know the project was successful if: at least 80% of attending youth complete a  post -  workshop     survey showing increased interest in using technology; participants can demonstrate   one new digital    skill; I receive positive feedback from at least one parent or community leader;  my team  completes   all steps without major unresolved issues.
