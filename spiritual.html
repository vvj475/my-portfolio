✝️ Christlike Attributes in My IT Career

Attribute How I Show It in My IT Work Scripture

⚠️ My Personal Warning Signs (When I Slip into a "Heart of War")

Warning Sign When It Happens How I Will Fix It
Defensiveness Someone challenges my technical solution. I will pause 10 seconds and say, "Help me understand your view."
Impatience A user learns slowly or asks the same question twice. I will remember how I struggled to learn IT concepts.
"Us vs. Them" thinking I see non-technical users as obstacles. I will remind myself each user is a child of God.
Cutting corners I am tempted to skip a security step to save time. I will re-read my ethical dilemma reflection.

📝 My Weekly Peace Audit

Every Friday, I will write three short answers. This keeps me growing and honest.

  1. Where did I show a heart of peace this week?
    Example: I stayed calm when a user was frustrated on a call...
  2. Where did a heart of war appear?
    Example: I felt annoyed during a long training session...
  3. What will I do differently next week?
    Example: I will take a deep breath before responding to difficult questions...

"In your patience possess ye your souls." — Luke 21:19

⚖️ My Ethical Dilemma Reflection

Scenario: An IT administrator accessed employee payroll and medical records without permission. He was "just curious" about a coworker's salary.

Why this is wrong:

  • It breaks trust. Employees trust IT staff to protect their data.
  • It treats people as objects. Viewing private information without consent disrespects the person.
  • Small wrongs lead to bigger wrongs. If I peek today, I might steal tomorrow.

My commitment: I will never abuse admin privileges. If tempted, I will ask: "Would I want someone doing this to my mother's records?"

Scripture anchor: "Let your light so shine before men" (Matthew 5:16)
